# Additional Questions CaseLinker Can Help Answer

Based on the data schema, analysis capabilities, and visualization features in CaseLinker, here are additional questions beyond those in the current evaluation form that the system can help answer:

## Case Type & Classification Questions

1. **How many cases involved production (creation of material) versus possession only?** What is the overlap between production and possession cases?

2. **How many cases involved hands-on contact versus online-only interactions?** What percentage of cases involve both hands-on and online components?

3. **How many cases are classified as multi-state or international?** What is the geographic scope distribution across cases?

4. **What is the distribution of case topics (production, possession, hands_on, online_digital, family, stranger, pornography)?** Which combinations of topics appear most frequently together?

5. **How many cases involve both production AND possession charges?** What is the relationship between these case types?

## Evidence & Content Analysis Questions

6. **What is the distribution of evidence volume across cases?** (e.g., number of images, videos, storage size, messages)

7. **What is the average and range of images/videos found in cases?** Are there patterns in evidence volume by case type?

8. **How many cases involved large-scale evidence (e.g., TB of storage, thousands of images)?** What case characteristics correlate with high evidence volumes?

9. **What is the relationship between evidence volume and case severity?** Do cases with more evidence tend to have higher severity indicators?

10. **How many cases involved message/chat evidence?** What platforms are most associated with message evidence?

## Severity & Victim Analysis Questions

11. **What is the distribution of severity indicators across all cases?** (infant, rape, very_young, under_10, production, physical_abuse)

12. **How many cases involve multiple severity indicators?** What combinations of severity indicators appear most frequently?

13. **What is the relationship between victim age and severity indicators?** Do younger victims correlate with specific severity indicators?

14. **How many cases involved multiple victims?** What is the distribution of victim counts (1, 2-5, 6-10, 10+)?

15. **What percentage of cases involve infant victims specifically?** How does this compare to very_young or under_10 categories?

16. **What is the distribution of severity phrases?** (dangerous, stated, told, continue, attacked, out_of_control) Which phrases correlate with higher priority cases?

## Perpetrator Analysis Questions

17. **What is the age distribution of perpetrators across cases?** Are there age patterns associated with specific case types or severity levels?

18. **How many perpetrators had previous convictions?** What is the relationship between previous convictions and case severity?

19. **What is the age distribution of perpetrators who were registered sex offenders versus non-registered offenders?** Are there age differences?

20. **What is the relationship between perpetrator age and victim age ranges?** Are there patterns in age gaps?

21. **How many cases involved perpetrators in positions of authority (teachers, family members)?** What is the breakdown by relationship type?

## Prosecution & Legal Outcomes Questions

22. **What is the distribution of prosecution outcomes?** (No Charges Listed, Sexual Exploitation of a Minor, Booked, Arrested, etc.)

23. **What are the most common charges filed across cases?** What is the frequency of each charge type?

24. **How many cases resulted in booking/jail time?** What case characteristics correlate with prosecution outcomes?

25. **What is the relationship between case severity and prosecution outcomes?** Do more severe cases have different prosecution patterns?

26. **Which agencies are most frequently involved in cases that result in prosecution?** Is there a correlation between agency involvement and outcomes?

## Investigation & Law Enforcement Questions

27. **What is the distribution of investigation types across all cases?** (proactive, reactive, online, undercover, unknown)

28. **Which law enforcement agencies are most frequently involved?** What is the frequency of each agency (AZICAC, FBI, Phoenix Police, ICAC, HSI, MCSO, DPS, etc.)?

29. **How many cases involved multiple agencies?** What are the most common agency collaboration patterns?

30. **What is the relationship between investigation type and case outcomes?** Do proactive investigations have different outcomes than reactive ones?

31. **Which investigation methods are most effective for different case types?** (e.g., proactive for online cases, reactive for family cases)

## Platform & Technology Questions

32. **What is the distribution of platforms used across cases?** Beyond the most common, what are the second, third, and fourth most common platforms?

33. **How many cases involved multiple platforms?** What are the most common platform combinations?

34. **What is the relationship between specific platforms and case types?** (e.g., Does Facebook correlate with family cases? Does Discord correlate with stranger cases?)

35. **How many cases involved online/digital environments versus physical contact?** What is the overlap?

36. **What platforms are most associated with production cases versus possession cases?**

## Relationship & Context Questions

37. **What is the breakdown of perpetrator relationships beyond family vs stranger?** (father, mother, brother, sister, uncle, aunt, cousin, teacher, stranger, unknown)

38. **How many cases involved family members specifically?** What is the breakdown by family relationship type?

39. **What is the relationship between perpetrator relationship and case severity?** Do family cases have different severity patterns than stranger cases?

40. **How many cases involved teachers specifically?** What are the characteristics of teacher-perpetrator cases?

41. **What is the relationship between relationship type and investigation method?** (e.g., Are family cases more likely to be reactive investigations?)

## Temporal & Trend Analysis Questions

42. **What are the temporal trends in case types over time?** (e.g., Has production increased? Has online_digital increased?)

43. **What are the trends in platform usage over time?** Are certain platforms becoming more or less common?

44. **What are the trends in investigation methods over time?** Has the distribution of proactive vs reactive changed?

45. **What is the case volume by year/month?** Are there seasonal or temporal patterns?

46. **What are the trends in severity indicators over time?** Has the proportion of high-severity cases changed?

## Cross-Category & Pattern Questions

47. **What is the relationship between registered sex offender status and case type?** (e.g., Are RSOs more likely to be involved in production or possession?)

48. **What is the relationship between registered sex offender status and investigation method?** Are RSOs more likely to be caught through proactive or reactive methods?

49. **What is the relationship between platform usage and investigation method?** (e.g., Are certain platforms more likely to be discovered through proactive investigations?)

50. **What is the relationship between evidence volume and prosecution outcomes?** Do cases with more evidence have better prosecution outcomes?

51. **What patterns exist between case topics and severity indicators?** (e.g., Do production cases have different severity patterns than possession cases?)

52. **What is the relationship between victim count and case type?** Do production cases tend to have more victims?

53. **What is the relationship between perpetrator age and platform usage?** Are younger perpetrators more likely to use certain platforms?

54. **What is the relationship between agency involvement and case outcomes?** Do certain agencies have better prosecution rates?

## Priority & Triage Questions

55. **What are the highest priority cases based on the automated priority scoring system?** What characteristics do they share?

56. **What is the distribution of priority scores across all cases?** How many cases fall into high (8-10), medium (6-8), and low (5-6) priority ranges?

57. **What combinations of factors lead to the highest priority scores?** (e.g., infant + production + multiple victims)

58. **How many cases have priority scores above 8.0?** What are their common characteristics?

## Clustering & Similarity Questions

59. **What are the most common case clusters identified by the similarity algorithm?** What characteristics define each cluster?

60. **How many cases are grouped together by similarity?** What is the average similarity score within clusters?

61. **What are the characteristics of cases that don't cluster with others?** Are there unique case patterns?

62. **What is the relationship between case similarity and geographic/temporal proximity?** Do similar cases occur close in time or location?

## Data Quality & Coverage Questions

63. **What percentage of cases have complete data for each field?** (e.g., How many cases have victim demographics? How many have evidence volume?)

64. **What are the most common missing data fields?** Which fields are most consistently populated?

65. **How many cases have data from each source organization?** (AZICAC, FBI, NCMEC, etc.) What is the data quality variation by source?

## Advanced Analysis Questions

66. **What keywords appear most frequently across case narratives?** What do these keywords reveal about case patterns?

67. **What are the automated insights generated by the system?** What patterns does the system detect automatically?

68. **What are the most common case topic combinations?** (e.g., production + hands_on, possession + online_digital)

69. **What severity indicator combinations appear most frequently?** (e.g., infant + production, very_young + hands_on)

70. **What are the characteristics of cases that appear in multiple clusters?** (e.g., cases that are both severe and involve production)

## How CaseLinker Can Answer These Questions

### Using Tag-Based Filtering (Analysis Page)
- Questions about specific case topics, severity indicators, platforms, investigation types, relationships, and RSO status can be answered by selecting relevant tags and viewing matching cases
- Multiple tags can be combined to find intersections (e.g., "possession" + "teacher" + "registered_sex_offender")

### Using Automated Analysis (Analysis Page)
- Priority and triage questions can be answered by viewing the "Top Priority Cases" section
- Clustering questions can be answered by viewing "Case Groups"
- Pattern detection questions can be answered by viewing "Automated Insights" and "Patterns Detected"
- Keyword analysis questions can be answered by viewing "Top Keywords"

### Using Visualizations (Visualization Page)
- Distribution questions (severity, outcomes, platforms, agencies, relationships) can be answered by viewing the interactive charts
- Timeline questions can be answered using the Timeline visualization
- Click-to-view functionality allows drilling down into specific cases

### Using Data Audit (Audit Page)
- Data quality and coverage questions can be answered by reviewing cases individually
- Field completeness can be assessed by browsing through cases

### Using Clusters Page
- Similarity and clustering questions can be answered by viewing case clusters and their characteristics
