## Why I Built CaseLinker

Hi, I'm Mrinaal. I built CaseLinker because I was struggling with understanding child exploitation cases. The challenges I faced were:

- **Fragmented data sources**: Cases are scattered across numerous organizations, states, and agencies, making it hard to see the bigger picture
- **Cross-case analysis**: Identifying patterns, similarities, and connections between cases was nearly impossible without manually reading through everything
- **Limitations in trend analysis**: I wanted to understand how child exploitation evolves, which platforms are being used, and what patterns emerge over time
- **Emotional impact**: Most importantly, repeatedly reading and processing highly disturbing case material is challenging

CaseLinker helps me by:
- **Providing actionable intelligence** without having to read every case detail
- **Showing case distributions and statistics** so I can understand patterns at a glance
- **Highlighting key information** when I do need to look at a case (like if I click "possession," it highlights possession-related phrases, or "infant" highlights infant-related phrases)
- **Enabling pattern analysis** across cases to identify trends, connections, and critical insights
- **Reducing emotional burden** by letting me work with structured data and visualizations instead of reading traumatic narratives

The goal of CaseLinker is to help researchers, analysts, and organizations gain actionable intelligence, understand the nature of these cases, see distributions and statistics, and identify other critical information—all without pressing any expand buttons. And if you do expand, the system highlights key information to minimize exposure to disturbing content.

---

## ⚠️ Important Note About Content

This system processes sensitive case data related to child exploitation and abuse. The content can be deeply disturbing. Everyone has different levels of comfort with this material, and that's completely okay.

**You do NOT need to expand cases or read case details to evaluate this system.** The entire evaluation can be completed using statistical views, visualizations, and structured data.

I will ask if you can use CaseLinker to answer certain key questions about the nature of these cases:

- **What organizations are involved?** Can you identify which law enforcement agencies handle these cases?
