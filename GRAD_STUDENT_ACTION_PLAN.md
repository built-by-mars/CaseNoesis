# Action Plan for Grad Student IRB Compliance

## ⚠️ Don't Panic - This is Fixable!

Your project uses **publicly available, de-identified data** which makes this relatively straightforward to resolve.

## Immediate Actions (Do These First)

### 1. **Contact Your IRB Office TODAY**
- **Email or call** your university's IRB office
- **Subject line**: "Quick Question: IRB Required for Public Data Analysis?"
- **Ask**: "I'm analyzing publicly available, de-identified case data from law enforcement reports. Do I need IRB review?"

### 2. **While Waiting for Response:**
- ✅ **Keep the site up** (don't take it down yet)
- ✅ **Document your data sources** (you already have this)
- ✅ **Prepare a brief description** of your project

### 3. **Prepare This Information for IRB:**

**Quick Summary to Send:**
```
Project: CaseLinker - Analysis of publicly available child exploitation case data

Data Source: Publicly available Arizona ICAC annual reports (2011-2014) 
and NCMEC reports that are already redacted and released for public consumption.

Data Type: Case summaries/narratives only - no PII, no direct identifiers

Research Activity: Secondary analysis of existing public data - no human 
subjects interaction, no data collection

Risk Level: Minimal - data is already public and de-identified
```

## What IRB Will Likely Say

### Most Likely Outcome: **Exempt Review** ✅
- **Why**: Public data analysis typically qualifies for exempt review
- **Timeline**: Usually 1-2 weeks for determination
- **Process**: Fill out a simple form, explain your project
- **Result**: You'll get an exemption letter

### Possible Outcome: **No IRB Needed** ✅
- Some universities don't require IRB for public data analysis
- They may say "this doesn't meet the definition of human subjects research"
- You'll get this in writing

### Unlikely but Possible: **Retroactive Approval** ✅
- If IRB says you need approval, you can apply retroactively
- Explain: "I didn't realize IRB was needed for public data"
- They'll review and likely approve (since data is public)

## Your Options Right Now

### Option 1: **Keep It Up, Get IRB Approval** (Recommended)
- ✅ Keep site running
- ✅ Contact IRB immediately
- ✅ Apply for exempt review
- ✅ Get approval (likely within 1-2 weeks)
- ✅ Document approval for future publications

**Pros**: 
- No interruption to your work
- Proper compliance
- Can use for thesis/publications

**Cons**: 
- Need to wait for IRB response
- May need to make minor adjustments

### Option 2: **Temporarily Restrict Access** (If Concerned)
- ✅ Add password protection or make it private
- ✅ Contact IRB
- ✅ Get approval
- ✅ Make it public again

**Pros**: 
- Reduces risk while waiting
- Shows you're being cautious

**Cons**: 
- Interrupts your work
- May be unnecessary

### Option 3: **Take It Down Temporarily** (If Very Concerned)
- ❌ Remove from public access
- ✅ Contact IRB
- ✅ Get approval
- ✅ Put it back up

**Pros**: 
- Maximum caution
- No public access during review

**Cons**: 
- Interrupts your work
- Likely unnecessary given public data

## Why You're Probably Fine

### ✅ **Low Risk Factors:**
1. **Public Data**: Already released by law enforcement
2. **No PII**: No names, addresses, or direct identifiers
3. **De-identified**: Data is already anonymized
4. **No Interaction**: You're not contacting anyone
5. **Secondary Analysis**: Analyzing existing summaries

### ✅ **This is Common:**
- Many grad students do similar projects
- IRBs see this type of research frequently
- Public data analysis is well-established
- Exempt review is standard for this

## What to Say to Your IRB Office

**Template Email:**

```
Subject: IRB Question: Public Data Analysis Project

Dear [IRB Office],

I'm a graduate student working on a research project analyzing publicly 
available case data. I want to ensure I'm in compliance with IRB requirements.

Project Description:
- Analyzing publicly available, de-identified case summaries from Arizona 
  ICAC annual reports (2011-2014) and NCMEC reports
- These reports are already redacted and released for public consumption
- No PII is processed; all data was already in the public domain
- No human subjects interaction; secondary analysis only

Question: Does this type of research require IRB review?

I want to ensure I'm following all requirements before proceeding further. 
Thank you for your guidance.

Best regards,
[Your Name]
[Your Department]
[Your Email]
```

## Timeline Expectations

- **Day 1**: Contact IRB office
- **Day 2-3**: IRB responds with guidance
- **Day 4-7**: If needed, submit exempt review form
- **Week 2**: Receive exemption/approval
- **Total**: Usually 1-2 weeks from contact to approval

## If IRB Says You Need Full Review

**Don't panic** - this is rare for public data, but if it happens:

1. **Submit application** - they'll guide you through it
2. **Emphasize**: Public data, no PII, no interaction
3. **Likely outcome**: Still approved, just takes longer (4-6 weeks)

## For Your Thesis/Publications

### ✅ **Good News:**
- IRB approval (even retroactive) is usually fine
- Many journals accept "IRB exempt" or "public data" statements
- You can note: "This research uses publicly available, de-identified data 
  and was determined to be exempt from IRB review" or "IRB exempt [protocol #]"

### ✅ **Document Everything:**
- Save IRB correspondence
- Keep exemption letter
- Document data sources
- Note approval date

## Red Flags to Watch For

### ⚠️ **If IRB Says:**
- "This requires full board review" → Ask why, emphasize public data
- "You need consent" → Explain it's public data, no subjects
- "You can't use this data" → Ask for clarification, show data source

### ✅ **If IRB Says:**
- "This is exempt" → Perfect! Get it in writing
- "No IRB needed" → Get it in writing
- "Submit exempt form" → Do it, it's quick

## Bottom Line

1. **Don't take it down** - likely unnecessary
2. **Contact IRB today** - get guidance
3. **Apply for exempt review** - if needed
4. **Get approval** - usually quick for public data
5. **Document everything** - for future use
