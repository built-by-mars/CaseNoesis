# What is an IRB?

## Simple Definition

**IRB = Institutional Review Board**

An IRB is a committee at universities and research institutions that reviews research projects to ensure they:
- Protect human subjects (people involved in research)
- Follow ethical guidelines
- Comply with federal regulations
- Minimize risks to participants

## Why IRBs Exist

IRBs were created because of past research abuses (like the Tuskegee Study). They ensure:
- ✅ Research is ethical
- ✅ Participants are protected
- ✅ Informed consent is obtained (when needed)
- ✅ Risks are minimized
- ✅ Benefits outweigh risks

## What IRBs Review

IRBs review research that involves:
- **Human subjects** (people participating in research)
- **Human data** (information about people)
- **Biological samples** (blood, tissue, etc.)
- **Interventions** (treatments, surveys, interviews)

## Types of IRB Review

### 1. **Exempt Review** (Fastest - 1-2 weeks)
- Minimal risk research
- Public data analysis (like yours!)
- Educational research
- Anonymous surveys
- **Your project likely falls here**

### 2. **Expedited Review** (2-4 weeks)
- Minimal risk but needs review
- Some data collection
- Some interventions

### 3. **Full Board Review** (4-8 weeks)
- Higher risk research
- Vulnerable populations
- Sensitive topics
- Complex protocols

## IRB Requirements

### When IRB Review is Required:
- ✅ Research at universities/institutions
- ✅ Research with human subjects
- ✅ Research with human data
- ✅ Research funded by federal grants
- ✅ Research for academic publication

### When IRB Review is NOT Required:
- ❌ Research on non-human subjects (animals, plants, objects)
- ❌ Some public data analysis (depends on institution)
- ❌ Independent research (not affiliated with institution)
- ❌ Journalism/news reporting
- ❌ Quality improvement (not research)

## How IRB Works

### The Process:
1. **You submit** an application describing your research
2. **IRB reviews** to determine risk level
3. **IRB decides**: Exempt, Expedited, or Full Review
4. **You get approval** (or feedback to revise)
5. **You conduct research** following approved protocol
6. **You report** any issues or changes

### The Application:
- Project description
- Data sources
- Methods
- Risk assessment
- Data security
- Privacy protections

## Common IRB Terms

- **Human Subject**: A living individual about whom you obtain data
- **PII (Personally Identifiable Information)**: Data that can identify someone
- **De-identified Data**: Data with identifiers removed
- **Informed Consent**: Permission from participants (not needed for public data)
- **Exempt**: Research that doesn't need ongoing IRB oversight
- **Protocol**: Your research plan/design
- **Amendment**: Changes to approved research

- ⚠️ **Future research** (may affect grants/funding)

## How to Find Your IRB

### At Your University:
1. **Search**: "[Your University] IRB" or "[Your University] Institutional Review Board"
2. **Check**: Research office, compliance office, or graduate school website
3. **Contact**: Usually an email or phone number listed
4. **Ask**: Your advisor or department - they'll know

### Common Locations:
- Office of Research Compliance
- Research Ethics Office
- Human Subjects Protection Office
- Graduate School (may have IRB info)
