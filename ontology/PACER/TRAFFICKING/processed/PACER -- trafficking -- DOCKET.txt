59  EO: On July 15, 2024, counsel for Defendant Darren Patrick Riley ("Defendant") filed a Motion to Continue Trial and Continue Hearing on Defendant's Motion to Dismiss ("Motion"), ECF No. 56 . The Motion seeks a
continuance of the trial date, even though Defendant "does not agree with counsel's request." Id. at PageID.283. The court thus construes the Motion as being brought by defense counsel over Defendant's objection.
The Motion, however, contains no discussion of the law, and specifically no case law on whether the court has the authority--when requested by defense counsel-- to: 1) continue a trial over a defendant's objection; and 2)
toll time under the Speedy Trial Act over a defendant's objection. Courts have found no Speedy Trial Act violation where counsel sought a continuance without consulting with a criminal defendant and obtaining his
consent beforehand. See, e.g., United States v. Robinson, 67 F.4th 742, 74849 (5th Cir. 2023). Here, however, the defendant has affirmatively objected.
The court also understands that defense counsel sought an expedited hearing on the Motion, which the court granted, setting the Motion for hearing on July 16, 2024. ECF No. 57 . Thus, counsel for Defendant is required
to provide the court, by midnight tonight (July 15, 2024), case law that addresses these questions. Counsel for the government may, but is not required, to do the same.
